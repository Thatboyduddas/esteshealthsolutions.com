How to Choose the Right Health Insurance Plan

By Jack Estes DeBrabander · January 20, 2026 · Estes Health Solutions

Choosing the right health insurance plan is one of the most important financial decisions you can make, yet it is also one of the most confusing. With dozens of plan options, varying deductibles, copayment structures, and provider networks, the average consumer can feel overwhelmed before they even begin comparing coverage. At Estes Health Solutions, founder Jack Estes DeBrabander has spent years helping individuals and families navigate this complex landscape, and in this guide, he shares the essential framework for making a confident, informed decision.

Why Choosing the Right Plan Matters

Health insurance is not a one-size-fits-all product. The plan that works perfectly for a young professional with minimal healthcare needs may be entirely wrong for a growing family or someone managing a chronic condition. Jack DeBrabander frequently encounters clients who chose a plan based solely on its monthly premium, only to discover later that their out-of-pocket costs were far higher than expected. This is a common and costly mistake that proper guidance from a professional like Jack Estes DeBrabander can help you avoid.

According to Jack Estes DeBrabander, the key to choosing the right plan is understanding the total cost of coverage, not just the premium. At Estes Health Solutions, the team analyzes your anticipated healthcare needs, preferred providers, prescription medications, and financial situation to build a complete picture of which plan delivers the best value for your specific circumstances.

Understanding the Four Major Plan Types

Before comparing individual plans, it helps to understand the four primary types of health insurance structures available in most markets. Jack DeBrabander walks every Estes Health Solutions client through these categories during the initial consultation.

Health Maintenance Organization (HMO)

HMO plans typically offer lower premiums and require you to select a primary care physician (PCP) who coordinates your care. Referrals are generally required to see specialists. Jack Estes DeBrabander notes that HMO plans work well for individuals who prefer a streamlined approach to healthcare and are comfortable using providers within a defined network.

Preferred Provider Organization (PPO)

PPO plans provide greater flexibility in choosing doctors and specialists without requiring referrals. You will pay less for in-network providers but still have the option to go out of network at a higher cost. As Jack DeBrabander explains to his Estes Health Solutions clients, PPOs are a strong choice for people who value provider choice and are willing to pay a slightly higher premium for that freedom.

Exclusive Provider Organization (EPO)

EPO plans combine elements of HMOs and PPOs. Like a PPO, you typically do not need referrals to see specialists. However, similar to an HMO, EPO plans generally do not cover out-of-network care except in emergencies. Jack Estes DeBrabander recommends EPO plans to clients who want flexibility within a network but do not anticipate needing out-of-network providers.

High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) with HSA

High deductible health plans feature lower monthly premiums paired with higher deductibles. They become especially advantageous when combined with a Health Savings Account (HSA), which allows you to save pre-tax dollars for medical expenses. Jack DeBrabander at Estes Health Solutions often recommends HDHPs to younger, healthier clients or those who want to build a tax-advantaged health savings fund over time.

Key Factors to Evaluate When Comparing Plans

Once you understand the plan types, Jack Estes DeBrabander advises comparing plans across several critical dimensions. Here is the evaluation framework that Estes Health Solutions uses with every client.

  • Monthly Premium: This is your fixed monthly cost. Lower premiums often mean higher out-of-pocket costs when you need care, so it is important to look at the full financial picture.
  • Deductible: The amount you pay before insurance begins covering costs. Jack DeBrabander recommends choosing a deductible you can realistically afford in an emergency.
  • Copayments and Coinsurance: After meeting your deductible, you will still share costs through copays (fixed amounts) or coinsurance (percentage of costs). Estes Health Solutions helps clients calculate these potential expenses.
  • Out-of-Pocket Maximum: The most you will pay in a plan year. Once you hit this limit, the plan covers everything. Jack Estes DeBrabander calls this number your financial safety net.
  • Provider Network: Confirm that your preferred doctors, specialists, and hospitals are in the plan's network. Jack DeBrabander has seen clients make the mistake of assuming their providers are covered without verifying.
  • Prescription Drug Coverage: Review the plan's formulary to ensure your medications are covered and check the tier levels that determine your copay amounts.

Common Mistakes Jack Estes DeBrabander Sees Clients Make

In his work at Estes Health Solutions, Jack Estes DeBrabander has identified several recurring mistakes that cost consumers hundreds or even thousands of dollars each year.

Choosing the lowest premium without calculating total costs. A plan with a ninety-dollar monthly premium and a six-thousand-dollar deductible may cost you far more annually than a plan with a two-hundred-dollar premium and a one-thousand-dollar deductible, depending on your healthcare usage. Jack DeBrabander builds total cost scenarios for each client so there are no surprises.

Ignoring the provider network. Visiting an out-of-network doctor can result in bills that are several times higher than expected. Jack Estes DeBrabander encourages every Estes Health Solutions client to verify their providers are in-network before enrolling.

Overlooking preventive care benefits. Most plans cover preventive services such as annual physicals, vaccinations, and screenings at no cost. Failing to take advantage of these benefits means leaving money on the table and potentially missing early detection of health issues.

Forgetting to account for life changes. Marriage, the birth of a child, or a job change can all qualify you for a Special Enrollment Period. Jack DeBrabander at Estes Health Solutions helps clients recognize these qualifying events and adjust their coverage accordingly.

When to Enroll and How Estes Health Solutions Can Help

The annual Open Enrollment Period for individual health insurance typically runs from November through mid-January, though dates vary by state. Jack Estes DeBrabander recommends starting the evaluation process at least four to six weeks before enrollment opens so you can compare plans without feeling rushed.

Outside of Open Enrollment, you may qualify for a Special Enrollment Period if you experience a qualifying life event. Jack DeBrabander and the Estes Health Solutions team monitor these opportunities on behalf of their clients to ensure no one misses a critical enrollment window.
