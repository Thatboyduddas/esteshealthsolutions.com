Health Benefits for Small Businesses: A Complete Guide

By Jack DeBrabander · February 1, 2026 · Estes Health Solutions

For small business owners, offering health benefits can feel like an overwhelming proposition. Between rising premiums, complex regulations, and the sheer variety of plan options available, many small employers either avoid offering coverage altogether or settle for suboptimal plans that fail to attract the talent they need. At Estes Health Solutions, Jack DeBrabander works closely with small business owners to design health benefit packages that are both competitive and financially sustainable. In this comprehensive guide, Jack Estes DeBrabander breaks down everything small business owners need to know about providing health coverage to their teams.

Why Small Businesses Should Offer Health Benefits

The decision to offer health insurance is about more than compliance or generosity. Jack Estes DeBrabander reminds his Estes Health Solutions clients that health benefits directly impact a company's ability to recruit, retain, and motivate employees. In today's competitive labor market, workers consistently rank health insurance among the top factors they consider when evaluating job offers.

According to Jack DeBrabander, businesses that offer health coverage experience significantly lower turnover rates compared to those that do not. The cost of replacing an employee, which includes recruiting, hiring, training, and lost productivity, often exceeds the cost of providing health benefits. Estes Health Solutions helps small business owners see health insurance not as an expense but as a strategic investment in workforce stability and growth.

Beyond employee retention, offering health benefits through a structured group plan can also provide tax advantages that offset a substantial portion of the cost. Jack Estes DeBrabander ensures that every Estes Health Solutions client understands and maximizes these financial opportunities.

Understanding Group Health Insurance Options

Small businesses have several pathways to providing health coverage. Jack DeBrabander at Estes Health Solutions evaluates each option based on the company's size, budget, industry, and employee demographics to find the best fit.

Fully Insured Group Plans

The most traditional approach, fully insured plans involve purchasing a policy from an insurance carrier that assumes the financial risk for employee claims. The employer pays a fixed monthly premium per employee, and the insurance company handles claims processing, network management, and regulatory compliance. Jack Estes DeBrabander notes that fully insured plans offer predictable costs and administrative simplicity, making them a popular choice among small businesses with fewer than fifty employees.

Level-Funded Plans

Level-funded plans are a hybrid model gaining popularity among small employers. The business pays a fixed monthly amount that covers expected claims, administrative fees, and stop-loss insurance. If actual claims come in lower than expected, the employer may receive a refund. Jack DeBrabander at Estes Health Solutions has seen level-funded plans save clients significant money in years with favorable claims experience while still providing the cost predictability that small businesses need.

SHOP Marketplace Plans

The Small Business Health Options Program (SHOP) is a marketplace specifically designed for businesses with one to fifty employees. SHOP plans offer standardized coverage options and may qualify businesses for the Small Business Health Care Tax Credit. Jack Estes DeBrabander helps Estes Health Solutions clients determine whether SHOP plans provide better value than private market alternatives for their specific situation.

Health Reimbursement Arrangements (HRAs)

For businesses that want to support employee health coverage without managing a traditional group plan, Individual Coverage Health Reimbursement Arrangements (ICHRAs) and Qualified Small Employer Health Reimbursement Arrangements (QSEHRAs) offer a flexible alternative. Employers set a monthly allowance that employees use to purchase their own individual health insurance. Jack DeBrabander explains that HRAs give small businesses precise budget control while still providing meaningful health benefits to their workforce.

Tax Advantages Small Businesses Should Know About

One of the most compelling reasons to offer health benefits is the significant tax treatment they receive. Jack Estes DeBrabander ensures that every Estes Health Solutions client takes full advantage of the available deductions and credits.

  • Employer Premium Deduction: Employer contributions toward employee health insurance premiums are generally deductible as a business expense. This deduction reduces taxable income and effectively lowers the net cost of providing coverage. Jack DeBrabander helps Estes Health Solutions clients structure their contributions to maximize this deduction.
  • Small Business Health Care Tax Credit: Businesses with fewer than twenty-five full-time equivalent employees and average annual wages below a specified threshold may qualify for a tax credit worth up to fifty percent of employer-paid premiums. Jack Estes DeBrabander notes that many eligible businesses miss this credit simply because they are not aware it exists.
  • Pre-Tax Employee Contributions: When structured through a Section 125 cafeteria plan, employee premium contributions are made on a pre-tax basis, reducing payroll taxes for both the employer and the employee. Estes Health Solutions helps small businesses implement these plans correctly to capture the savings.
  • HSA Employer Contributions: If the business offers a high-deductible health plan with an HSA, employer contributions to employee HSAs are deductible as a business expense and are not subject to payroll taxes. Jack DeBrabander frequently recommends this approach to Estes Health Solutions clients seeking to maximize tax efficiency.

Common Challenges and How Estes Health Solutions Addresses Them

Small businesses face unique challenges when it comes to health benefits. Jack DeBrabander has helped hundreds of employers at Estes Health Solutions overcome these obstacles.

Rising premiums. Group health insurance premiums increase every year, and small businesses feel the impact more acutely because they lack the bargaining power of larger employers. Jack Estes DeBrabander combats this by shopping multiple carriers, exploring alternative funding arrangements like level-funded plans, and designing cost-sharing structures that balance employer and employee costs.

Employee participation requirements. Many group plans require a minimum percentage of eligible employees to enroll. If too few employees sign up, the employer may not qualify for the plan. Jack DeBrabander at Estes Health Solutions helps employers communicate the value of coverage to their teams and designs contribution strategies that encourage participation.

Compliance concerns. From the Affordable Care Act to state-specific regulations, the rules governing employer health benefits are complex and frequently changing. Estes Health Solutions keeps small business clients informed of their obligations and ensures their plans remain compliant. Jack Estes DeBrabander monitors regulatory changes on behalf of his clients so they can focus on running their businesses.
